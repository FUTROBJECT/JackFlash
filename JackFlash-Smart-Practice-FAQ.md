# How JackFlash Picks What to Practice

## "Does my child just get random problems?"

No. JackFlash uses a smart practice engine inspired by Singapore Math's mastery-based approach and spaced repetition research. Every problem your child sees is chosen deliberately based on what they know, what they're learning, and what they're ready for next.

## "What does 'smart practice' actually mean?"

Behind the scenes, JackFlash sorts every math fact into one of five categories and adjusts how often each one appears:

**New facts** are introduced gradually — only 2 or 3 at a time. Your child won't be overwhelmed by dozens of unfamiliar problems all at once. Once they start getting the current batch right, new facts are introduced automatically.

**Learning facts** are ones your child has gotten right before but hasn't fully mastered yet. These show up frequently so momentum isn't lost.

**Struggling facts** are the ones your child has seen multiple times but keeps getting wrong. These get the most attention — the app prioritizes them above everything else so your child gets the repetition they need right where they need it.

**Mastered facts** don't disappear. They come back for review on a schedule that gets longer over time (see below). This prevents the "learned it, forgot it" problem.

**Review-due facts** are mastered facts whose review interval has come up. They get mixed back into practice so your child stays sharp on material they've already learned.

## "How does mastery work?"

A fact is considered mastered after your child answers it correctly 3 times. But mastery isn't permanent — if they get a mastered fact wrong during a review, it drops back into the learning pool and needs to be re-mastered. This keeps the standard honest.

## "What is spaced repetition?"

Spaced repetition is a learning technique backed by decades of cognitive science research. The idea is simple: review material at gradually increasing intervals.

Here's how JackFlash applies it:

- A newly mastered fact comes back for review **the next day**
- Get it right again? It waits **3 days**
- Right again? **7 days**
- Then **14 days**
- Then **30 days**

Each successful review stretches the interval. If your child gets it wrong at any point, the fact resets and goes back into regular practice. This cycle ensures facts move from short-term memory into long-term retention.

## "Why doesn't my child see all the facts right away?"

This is by design. Singapore Math emphasizes mastery before moving on — students should own a small set of facts before new ones are introduced. JackFlash limits new fact introduction to a few at a time so your child builds confidence and fluency on what's in front of them before the pool expands.

## "What happens when my child gets an answer wrong?"

Two things: the fact's mastery level drops by one (so it shows up more often going forward), and the visual scaffold appears automatically — the dot array or bar model that helps your child see the math, not just guess. On the next visit, they have another chance to build toward mastery.

## "How is this different from flashcard apps?"

Most flashcard apps show facts randomly or in a fixed sequence. JackFlash adapts in real time to your child's actual performance. A child who has mastered their 2s and 5s but struggles with 7s and 8s will see far more 7s and 8s — they won't waste time drilling facts they already know. And when those 2s and 5s are due for review, they'll appear just often enough to keep them fresh.

## "Can I see which facts my child is struggling with?"

Yes. The Progress view (tap the chart icon during practice) shows mastery dots for every fact. In the Parent Zone, the "Needs Practice" section lists the specific facts with the lowest mastery levels — including ones your child hasn't attempted yet.

## "Does the app track anything about my child online?"

No. All practice data stays on your device. Nothing is sent to a server, no accounts are required, and no personal information is collected. See our Privacy Policy for details.
